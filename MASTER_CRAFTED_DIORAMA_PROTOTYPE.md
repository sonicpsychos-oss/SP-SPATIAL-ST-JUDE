# The Master-Crafted Diorama Visual Prototype

## Project Context
**Client:** Project St. Jude / SP Spatial  
**Audience:** Executive board, donors, and patient families  
**Primary Outcome:** Three high-impact mockups proving:
1. Soft Realism
2. Context-Aware LOD
3. Cross-Platform Feasibility

---

## Visual North Star

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme:** Tactile Architectural Model
- **Tone:** Warm, clean, approachable, grounded
- **Avoid:** Sterile, blue-tinted CAD realism

### Lighting Direction
- **Color temperature:** 3200K–4500K
- **Mood:** “Dawn of Life” golden-hour sunlight
- **Shadows:** Soft and elongated
- **Contrast:** No harsh digital blacks

### Material & Edge Language
- Subtle bevel/chamfer treatment on all major edges
- Physical-model readability over hyper-photoreal noise
- Tactile PBR response for brick, glass, and stone in beauty views

---

## Hero Cluster Scope

### Required Scene Contents
- **Primary building (choose one):**
  - 15-story Clinical Tower **or**
  - Inspiration4 ARC building
- **Support context:**
  - Family Commons garden segment (biophilic)
  - One sky-bridge
  - Low-poly grey-box Memphis city extrusions from GIS massing

### Scope Discipline
- Build one hero cluster only (no full-campus modeling)
- Emphasize repeatable modular kit-bashing:
  - One window module
  - One wall panel module
  - One sky-bridge segment module

---

## Required Deliverables: The 3 Killer Frames

## Frame 1 — Donor Reveal (Macro/Desktop)

### Composition
- High-angle tilt-shift framing
- Shallow depth of field focused on hero building and immediate commons

### Visual Quality Targets
- High-density foliage in hero foreground/midground
- Soft-baked GI look
- Premium PBR texturing (brick/glass/stone)

### Signature Technique
- Apply **Interior Mapping (cubemap-based)** on window surfaces to suggest warm occupied rooms without interior geometry.

### Intent
- Emotional reaction target: “I want to touch this.”

---

## Frame 2 — Trauma-Informed Pivot (Split-Screen Mobile)

### Layout
- Side-by-side split in mobile aspect ratio
- Left: exploration-mode crop of Frame 1
- Right: same building in focus-mode abstraction

### Right-Side Focus Mode Rules
- Remove cast shadows
- Remove foliage detail
- Replace detailed textures with matte paper-model whites/beiges
- Overlay a high-contrast **Pantone 108C yellow** navigation path from parking garage to main entrance

### Intent
- Demonstrate reduction of cognitive load for stressed caregivers/parents.

---

## Frame 3 — Feasibility Mic-Drop (Technical Triptych)

### Panel A: Beauty Pass
- Full soft-realism textured render of hero cluster

### Panel B: Topology Pass (Wireframe)
- Optimized low-poly topology visualization
- Clean quads prioritized
- No chaotic CAD/Revit triangulation artifacts

### Panel C: Fabrication Pass (3D Print)
- Monochrome watertight “clay” representation
- Add printer-nozzle overlay/annotation to indicate fabrication readiness

### Intent
- Prove production fitness and system stability for downstream teams.

---

## Technical Constraints & Brand Safety

### Color Governance
- **Do not use red paths.**
- St. Jude red (Pantone 193C) reserved for logo usage only.

### Performance Targets
- Texture workflow constrained to **2K atlases** to demonstrate mobile WebGL viability.

### Pipeline Proof
- Modular repetition should be visible and deliberate to validate procedural assembly logic.
